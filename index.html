Oban

Founded as a fishing village, this picturesque town is home to the Museum of Lobster Pots. Its main industry is now tourism, although other manufacturing and service industries continue. Recreational vessels are now moored in the harbour, and a golf course is situated near the town. Many businesses operate sightseeing and wildlife cruises from the harbour, from April to October. An abundance of other wildlife, including seal colonies, also inhabit the nearby islands. The town has a parish church at its centre that is perched on a small hill. This structure incorporates a tower/spire feature rare to Britain, but common to the area.
